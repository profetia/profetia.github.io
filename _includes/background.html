Bio

My research interest spans broadly in computer systems, including high performance computing, operating systems and distributed systems. My research focuses on building high performance and reliable systems.

I obtained my B.E. degree in Computer Science from ShanghaiTech University in July 2025. During my undergraduate years, I was fortunate to work with Prof. Yiting Xia at Max Planck Institute for Informatics, focusing on optical data center networks. I also worked with Prof. Zhice Yang at ShanghaiTech on asymmetric multi-core systems scheduling and Prof. Zhihao Jiang on formal verification for medical diagnosis. Moreover, I was the leader of Geekpie HPC during 2023-2024, actively participating in student cluster competitions and providing public services (e.g. ShanghaiTech Mirror).

Prior to UIUC, I was a HPC infrastructure engineer at Ubiquant, where I worked on building high performance storage systems for LLM training and inference. I also interned at Tencent as a system software developer in 2024. I'm also a Rust enthusiast and a regular contributor to Rust Clippy, where I helped triage issues and give bug fixes and lint improvements.

When I'm not in front of a computer screen, I enjoy swimming, cooking, video games and traveling.
